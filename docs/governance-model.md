# Governance Model

## Objective

Establish clear decision rights, escalation paths, meeting cadence, and documentation standards for nonprofit fundraising operations and supporting systems work.

## Governance Principles

- decisions should have a named owner
- process exceptions should be visible, not informal
- reporting definitions should be stable across periods
- operational work and governance oversight should remain distinct

## Decision Rights

| Decision Area | Primary Owner | Approver | Consulted |
|---|---|---|---|
| fundraising goals | executive leadership | board | development lead, finance lead |
| campaign launch | development lead | executive leadership | CRM admin, finance lead |
| sponsorship package changes | sponsorship lead | executive leadership | marketing, finance |
| data standards | CRM admin | executive leadership | development lead |
| dashboard definitions | reporting owner | executive leadership | finance lead, board lead |
| system changes | project lead | executive sponsor | affected users |

## Stakeholder Roles

### Executive Sponsor
Provides direction, clears blockers, and approves major changes.

### Development Lead
Owns fundraising process performance and campaign execution.

### Sponsorship Lead
Owns sponsor pipeline, package strategy, and fulfillment tracking.

### CRM Admin
Owns system configuration standards, data quality controls, and reporting support.

### Finance Lead
Owns reconciliation, revenue validation, and financial control alignment.

### Board or Committee Lead
Provides governance oversight and monitors progress to target.

## Escalation Model

Escalate when any of the following occurs:
- revenue risk exceeds agreed threshold
- reporting totals do not reconcile
- campaign launch date is at risk
- sponsor deliverables are likely to be missed
- data quality issue affects decision-making
- ownership is unclear for a critical task

## Change Control

Use lightweight change control for:
- stage definition changes
- dashboard metric changes
- campaign hierarchy changes
- required field changes
- role or ownership changes
- automation or validation rule updates

### Minimum Change Record
- change requested
- reason for change
- impacted process or report
- owner
- approval decision
- effective date

## Meeting Cadence

| Meeting | Cadence | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| pipeline review | weekly | review prospects, asks, and next steps |
| sponsorship review | weekly during selling period | review sponsor progress and blockers |
| reconciliation review | monthly | align CRM and finance totals |
| governance review | monthly or board aligned | review KPI progress, risks, and decisions |
| implementation working session | weekly during projects | resolve open actions and design items |

## Issue and Risk Management

### Issues
Current problems requiring action now.

### Risks
Potential problems that may affect goals, timing, reporting, or delivery.

Track both with:
- description
- owner
- impact
- mitigation
- due date
- status

## Documentation Expectations

Maintain current versions of:
- process workflows
- stage definitions
- dashboard metric definitions
- data standards
- key templates
- issue and risk logs
- decision records

## Success Indicators

The governance model is working when:
- decisions happen without confusion on ownership
- reporting stays consistent month to month
- issues are surfaced early
- major process changes are documented before rollout
- finance and fundraising teams are aligned on revenue views
