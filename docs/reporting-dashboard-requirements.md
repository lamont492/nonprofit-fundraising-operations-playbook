# Reporting Dashboard Requirements

## Objective

Define practical dashboard requirements for nonprofit fundraising operations so leadership, staff, and boards can make decisions from consistent data.

## Dashboard 1: Executive Summary Dashboard

### Audience
Executive leadership and fundraising leadership.

### Purpose
Provide a concise view of fundraising progress, pipeline health, and current risks.

### Sample Metrics
- total raised versus goal
- cash received this month
- pledge balance outstanding
- active opportunities by stage
- sponsor revenue versus target
- donor retention rate
- top campaign performance

### Source Data
- CRM opportunities
- campaign records
- payments and pledge schedules
- accounting summary totals if applicable

### Refresh Cadence
Weekly during active campaigns. Monthly outside peak periods.

### Common Pitfalls
- mixing committed revenue with cash received
- inconsistent campaign naming
- stale opportunity stages

## Dashboard 2: Campaign Dashboard

### Audience
Development staff and campaign owners.

### Purpose
Monitor the performance of a single campaign or appeal.

### Sample Metrics
- campaign revenue versus target
- donor count
- average gift size
- response rate
- conversion rate
- campaign member status distribution

### Source Data
- campaign members
- opportunities linked to campaign
- outreach activity logs

### Refresh Cadence
Twice weekly during active solicitations.

### Common Pitfalls
- gifts not linked to the right campaign
- campaign member statuses not maintained
- duplicate contacts inflating counts

## Dashboard 3: Donor Pipeline Dashboard

### Audience
Development leadership, gift officers, and executive sponsors.

### Purpose
Track prospect movement, next steps, and solicitation readiness.

### Sample Metrics
- active prospects by stage
- upcoming asks in next 30 days
- prospects without next step
- committed versus open pipeline
- pipeline value by owner

### Source Data
- opportunities
- tasks and activities
- owner assignments
- expected close dates

### Refresh Cadence
Weekly.

### Common Pitfalls
- opportunities left open after disposition
- no standardized stage definitions
- next steps tracked outside system

## Dashboard 4: Sponsorship Dashboard

### Audience
Sponsorship lead, event lead, and finance support.

### Purpose
Track sponsor acquisition, payment progress, and fulfillment readiness.

### Sample Metrics
- sponsor revenue versus target
- commitments versus cash received
- sponsor count by tier
- open sponsor prospects
- fulfillment completion rate
- renewal rate

### Source Data
- sponsor accounts and contacts
- sponsorship opportunities
- fulfillment tracker fields
- payment records

### Refresh Cadence
Weekly before event. Twice weekly in final month before event.

### Common Pitfalls
- sponsor benefits tracked outside system
- no clear distinction between commitment and payment
- fulfillment tasks not assigned

## Dashboard 5: Board Reporting Dashboard

### Audience
Board members and finance committee.

### Purpose
Provide a governance-level view of fundraising progress without operational noise.

### Sample Metrics
- year to date fundraising versus goal
- major campaign status
- donor retention trend
- number of new donors
- sponsorship revenue progress
- forecast to target
- top risks and mitigation items

### Source Data
- summarized CRM reporting
- reconciled finance totals
- risk and issue logs

### Refresh Cadence
Monthly or aligned to board meeting schedule.

### Common Pitfalls
- overloading the board with transaction-level detail
- presenting unreconciled data
- changing metric definitions mid-year

## Data Governance Recommendations

- define one owner per dashboard
- document metric definitions in advance
- reconcile key financial totals before publication
- separate operational dashboards from governance dashboards
- archive prior period views for trend analysis
