# Sponsorship Strategy Framework

## Objective

Provide a repeatable framework for designing sponsorship offers, targeting prospects, managing outreach, tracking fulfillment, and improving renewal outcomes.

## Sponsorship Model Components

### 1. Package Design
Define sponsorship levels with a clear exchange of value.

Include:
- price point
- inventory limits
- recognition benefits
- audience access benefits
- hospitality benefits
- mission impact narrative

### 2. Targeting Strategy
Segment prospects into practical tiers.

| Tier | Profile | Typical Ask Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | existing supporters and warm corporate relationships | personalized outreach with leadership involvement |
| Tier 2 | local businesses aligned to audience and geography | tailored email plus follow-up sequence |
| Tier 3 | broad prospect list | scaled outreach with qualification gate |

### 3. Outreach Sequence
Recommended sequence:
1. personalized introduction
2. mission and event relevance note
3. sponsorship deck or landing page share
4. follow-up within 3 to 5 business days
5. escalation to leadership or board relationship owner if warm path exists
6. closeout with final availability note

## Targeting Criteria

Evaluate prospects against:
- brand alignment
- geographic relevance
- employee or community footprint
- history of local giving
- prior event support
- likelihood of renewing annually

## Package Design Principles

- keep levels simple enough to compare quickly
- limit top tiers to create urgency
- connect benefits to real visibility, not vague promises
- show what the funding supports
- separate recognition benefits from operational deliverables

## Follow Up Logic

| Scenario | Next Step |
|---|---|
| no response after first outreach | send concise follow-up with relevance reminder |
| interest but no decision | confirm timeline and hold inventory for a defined period |
| asks for deck | send deck with one clear next action |
| asks for custom option | assess internal feasibility before offering exceptions |
| declines | capture reason and future timing |

## Fulfillment Tracking Requirements

Track each committed sponsor against:
- signed agreement or documented commitment
- invoice status
- payment received date
- logo received
- recognition placement completed
- tickets or access delivered
- on-site deliverables completed
- post-event thank you sent

## Post Event Stewardship

Within 2 weeks of event completion:
- send thank you note
- share photos or recap if available
- confirm benefits delivered
- document issues or misses
- schedule renewal outreach timing

## KPI Examples

- outreach volume by tier
- response rate
- meeting rate
- conversion rate
- average sponsorship value
- sponsor renewal rate
- fulfillment completion rate
- sponsor revenue by relationship source

## Common Failure Points

- unclear package differentiation
- outreach not tied to relationship ownership
- sponsor commitments tracked outside primary system
- delayed logo collection and recognition production
- no post-event stewardship process

## Recommended Operating Cadence

- weekly pipeline review during selling period
- twice-weekly check on committed sponsor fulfillment close to event date
- post-event retrospective within 10 business days
